LOCAL EXPERIENCES

Akshardham Temple

Heralded by the Guinness World Records as the World's Largest Comprehensive Hindu Temple, this temple epitomizes 10,000 years of Indian culture.

Lotus Temple

An architectural marvel, the Bohai temple is built in the shape of a lotus flower and made of pure white marble.

Gurudwara Bangla Sahib

The most prominent Sikh house of worship in Delhi; the gurudwara grounds include the temple, a kitchen, a school, an art gallery and a large pond which is considered holy by the Sikhs.

Jama Masjid

Built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan, this highly decorative mosque has three great gates, four towers and two 40 metre-high minarets constructed of strips of red sandstone and white marble.

India Gate

New Delhi's definitive monument, this Arc-de-Triomphe-like archway commemorates the 70,000 Indian soldiers who lost their lives fighting for the British Army during World War I.

Red Fort

The red sandstone walls of the massive Red Fort Or Lal Qila rise 33 metres above the clamour of Old Delhi. Inside is a veritable treasure trove of buildings including the Drum House, the Hall of Public Audiences, the white marble Hall of Private Audiences, the Pearl Mosque, Royal Baths and the Palace of Colour.

Humayun's Tomb

Built in 1565 A.D. this magnificent garden tomb is the first substantial example of Mughal architecture in India.

Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar is a soaring, 73 metre-high tower of victory, built in 1193 by Qutab-ud-din Aibak.

Jantar Mantar

Constructed in 1724 by Maharaja Jai Singh of Jaipur; it consists of 13 architectural astronomy instruments.

Lodi Gardens

Spread over 90 acres, the Lodi Gardens were designed in 0444 by one of the ruling Mughal dynasties in India. The Indian National Trust for Art and Cultural Heritage (INTACH) and the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI) organize heritage walks within the park area.

National Zoological Park

Spread over 204 acres, the park houses more than 2,000 species of animals and birds and approximately 200 varieties of flora.

Budhha Garden

The Buddha Gardens are beautifully decorated with manicured lawns, streams, landscaped terrain and large canopied trees.

Garden of Five Senses

The Garden of Five Senses is not just a park; it is a space with a variety of activities inviting public interaction and exploration. Majesticrocks stand silhouetted against the sky, others lie strewn upon the ground in a casual yet alluring display of nature's sculptural genius.

Dilli Haat

Presents a unique experience of Indian artand heritage set against a fascinating panorama of craft, cuisine and cultural activities. It provides the ambiance of a traditional rural haat or village market, with stalls selling handicrafts from all corners of the country.

Khan Market

An upscale shopping area for jewellery, delicatessens, restaurants and bookstores.

Connaught Place

Designed by Edwin Lutyens in the shape of a horse-shoe, CP is an upscale commercial district with corporate offices, restaurants and handloom emporiums.

Chandni Chowk

The old city's famed shopping strip; enjoy a colourful bustling street market experience.

Janpath

Street market famous for trinkets, loots"(Indian handmdde leather shoes) and traditional handicraft items.
